SpeakUP AI — Public Speaking Training Structure
================================================

PROGRESSION SYSTEM
------------------
Same AI-driven system as Debate:
  - Student starts at a level based on onboarding experience
  - AI evaluates after every practice session
  - Verdict: Advance / Stay / Drop (drop is rare)
  - Level stored in localStorage under "speakup_speaking_progress"
  - XP per session: upload=20, event_practice=15, articulation_drill=10

LEVEL 1 — Foundations of Speech
---------------------------------
Who it's for: Students who have never formally practiced public speaking
Goal: Speak clearly, confidently, and with basic structure

Topics covered:
  1. Why public speaking matters — and why most people fear it
  2. Articulation basics — enunciation, clarity, filler words (um, uh, like)
  3. Vocal variety — pace, pitch, pause, and volume
  4. Basic speech structure — Opening Hook, Body, Closing Call to Action
  5. Body language fundamentals — posture, eye contact, hand gestures
  6. Overcoming nervousness — breathing, grounding, reframing anxiety
  7. The 1-minute rule — how to say something meaningful in 60 seconds
  8. Practice: Deliver a 60-second self-introduction

AI Coach focus:
  - Warm and encouraging
  - Celebrates first attempts
  - Focuses on one improvement at a time
  - Never overwhelms with too much feedback

Route:   /public-speaking/level-1
API:     /api/public-speaking/level-1
XP per session: 15-20


LEVEL 2 — Style & Delivery
----------------------------
Who it's for: Students who can speak clearly but want to be compelling
Goal: Develop a personal speaking style and hold a room's attention

Topics covered:
  1. Finding your speaking voice — authenticity vs. performance
  2. Advanced articulation — consonant clarity, vowel precision, rhythm
  3. Storytelling structure — the problem/journey/resolution arc
  4. Persuasive techniques — Ethos (credibility), Pathos (emotion), Logos (logic)
  5. Audience management — reading the room, adapting in real time
  6. Advanced body language — movement, space, purposeful gestures
  7. Handling silence — using pauses as power, not weakness
  8. Practice: 3-minute persuasive speech on a chosen topic

AI Coach focus:
  - Pushes for more personality and risk-taking
  - Identifies when delivery feels generic or flat
  - Challenges students to be more specific and vivid
  - Compares to what a compelling speaker would do differently

Route:   /public-speaking/level-2
API:     /api/public-speaking/level-2
XP per session: 18-25


LEVEL 3 — Performance & Events
--------------------------------
Who it's for: Students preparing for real speaking events or competitions
Goal: Deliver polished, event-specific speeches under pressure

Topics covered:
  1. Adapting to different speaking contexts (formal, casual, competitive)
  2. Event-specific structure and expectations (see Events section below)
  3. Advanced persuasion — framing, emotional arc, narrative tension
  4. Q&A and live audience handling
  5. Pressure performance — maintaining composure under evaluation
  6. Vocal stamina for longer speeches
  7. Speech opening mastery — the first 10 seconds decide everything
  8. Practice: Full event simulation with judge-level feedback

AI Coach focus:
  - Evaluates at a real audience/judge standard
  - Identifies what separates good from great
  - Focuses on polish, specificity, and originality
  - Gives competition/event-ready assessment

Route:   /public-speaking/level-3
API:     /api/public-speaking/level-3
XP per session: 20-30


SPECIFIC EVENTS (available at all levels, difficulty scales)
-------------------------------------------------------------

  EVENT                     WHAT IT TESTS
  ---------                 -------------------------
  Self-Introduction         Clarity, warmth, memorability (60 sec)
  Class Presentation        Structure, evidence, engagement (3-5 min)
  Valedictorian Speech      Story, inspiration, personal voice (3-4 min)
  TEDx-Style Talk           Big idea, narrative arc, delivery (5-7 min)
  Persuasive Speech         Ethos/Pathos/Logos, call to action (3-5 min)
  Informative Speech        Clarity, organization, audience awareness (3-5 min)
  Debate Opening Statement  Framing, argument preview, confidence (2-3 min)
  Model UN Opening Speech   Formal language, country representation (1-2 min)
  College Interview Answer  Concise, specific, authentic (60-90 sec)
  Elevator Pitch            Hook, value prop, ask (30-60 sec)

Each event template includes:
  - What the judges/audience care about most
  - A structure guide specific to that event
  - Example opening lines for inspiration
  - Common mistakes to avoid
  - The AI feedback lens calibrated to that event type


FEATURES
--------

1. SPEECH UPLOAD + AI FEEDBACK  ← main feature
   - Student types or pastes their speech text
   - AI evaluates across 6 dimensions:
       Articulation  — clarity, word choice, filler word detection
       Structure     — opening, body, close, logical flow
       Style         — personality, originality, voice
       Delivery      — pace, energy, pausing (inferred from text)
       Persuasion    — ethos/pathos/logos balance
       Confidence    — assertiveness, hedging language, conviction
   - Returns: score per dimension, specific feedback, improved version
   - Session recorded for weakness tracking

2. EVENT PRACTICE
   - Student picks an event type
   - AI provides the event brief (what it is, what's expected)
   - Student writes their speech for that event
   - AI evaluates at event-specific standard
   - Returns judge-style feedback tailored to the event

3. ARTICULATION DRILL (Level 1 & 2 focus)
   - AI gives a tongue twister or articulation exercise
   - Student attempts to write out the words in clear, precise form
   - Checks for clarity, rhythm, and difficult sound combinations
   - Timed challenge (30 seconds)

4. WEAKNESS DETECTOR (same as debate)
   - Tracks patterns across sessions
   - Shows top weaknesses and drills to fix them
   - Updates after every session

5. AI PROGRESSION JUDGE
   - After XP hits 100%, AI evaluates sessions
   - Checks: articulation improvement, structure consistency, style development
   - Verdict: Advance / Stay / Drop
   - Explains decision to student
